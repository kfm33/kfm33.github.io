The Place Where Stillness Feels Safe

There is a woodland clearing that does not ask you to arrive calm.

You can arrive restless.

You can arrive with your thoughts racing ahead of you.

You can arrive already tired from holding too much.

The clearing does not mind.

The first thing you notice is the ground.

It is firm. It will not give way.

You choose a place to sit â€” a smooth stone or a fallen log.

You do not have to close your eyes. You do not have to breathe in any special way.

You simply sit.

At first, your thoughts are louder than the birds.

That is alright.

After a little while, a robin lands nearby.

It hops once. Twice. Then pauses.

You notice the pause.

You do not have to copy it.

You only notice it.

Some time passes.

A red squirrel moves through the branches overhead.

It stops. It listens. It moves again.

You notice the pattern: movement, pause, movement.

Your shoulders lower a fraction without you deciding to.

The air feels cooler now.

Further away, at the edge of the trees, a roe deer steps forward.

It is not rushing. It is not frozen.

It stands in quiet balance.

You notice your breathing has found a steadier rhythm.

Nothing dramatic has happened.

You have not solved anything.

But you are still here.

And because you are still here, the woodland reveals a little more.

As the light softens, an owl takes its place above.

It does not call immediately.

It waits.

The longer you remain, the more the clearing feels different.

Not silent.

But settled.

Your anxiety has not been pushed away.

It has simply slowed enough to sit beside you instead of ahead of you.

Stillness is not empty.

It is full.

That is the reward.

You are here. That is enough.
